How to evaluate a 20W AI server before buying

If you are comparing options, focus on a few factors that affect daily use more than flashy marketing does.

1. Power efficiency in real use

Look for actual operating power, not just a theoretical peak. A system designed around roughly 15W to 20W is easier to keep online all the time and easier to cool quietly.

2. AI acceleration

Raw CPU only devices often disappoint. Dedicated AI acceleration changes the experience dramatically, especially for local assistants and inference heavy workflows.

3. Storage

Local AI gets more pleasant when the system has fast NVMe storage. It helps with models, logs, indexes, and overall responsiveness.

4. Software readiness

Hardware alone does not solve much. A device with a mature software stack and pre installed automation environment saves time and reduces friction.

5. Total cost

Do not compare hardware price alone. Compare the total cost of ownership, including electricity, time spent setting it up, and how often cloud tools would still be required.
